When & When Can Taurus Be Seen?

Taurus can be seen in the nothern hemisphere between Fall and Spring, and can be seen in the sourthern hemisphere between Spring and Fall.

Story

In Greek mythology, Taurus is often associated with Zeus. According the the myth, once upon a time, Zeus grew attracted to Europa, who was a Phoenician princess and the daughter of King Agenor of Phoenicia and Telephassa. Because of this, Zeus appeared in front of her as an attractive white bull named Taurus. Europa eventually fell for bull (Zeus) and climed on its back. Zeus then took Europa to the beach and jumped in the water. After that, Zeus took Europa to Crete. There, he took the form of an eagle and had 3 sons with Europa.

Interesting facts

  • Some of the also have traditional names. For example,:
    • α Tauri is traditionally called Aldebaran
    • β Tauri is traditionally called Elnath
    • γ Tauri is traditionally called Prima-Hyadum
  • The Taurus constallation has some of the the brightest stars out of any constallation.
  • Aldebaran is the brightest star in the Taurus constallation, having an absolute magnitude value of −0.641±0.034.
  • Taurus is one of the oldest constellations known, dating back to at least the Early Bronze Age
  • The Taurus constellation contains a binary star system named LP 413-53AB. It is very small, consisting of 2 ultracool dwarf stars. These stars are very close to each other, and because of that, they orbit each other every 20.5 hours.
  • The Crab Nebula, or Messier 1, contains a pulsar discovered in 1968. This pulsar helped explain the brightness of the nebula.
  • Taurus is one of the signs of the zodiac and is important in many cultures.
  • Alpha Tauri, also known as Aldebaran is often called the "Bull’s fiery eye" because of its location, apparent magnitude, and color.
